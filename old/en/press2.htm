Ready to withstand Helkern 270 days before it came

Safety-Lab www.safety-lab.com confirms that its flagship product, Shadow Database Scanner, was ready to detect vulnerability that made possible the pandemic spread of Helkern Internet worm (Worm.SQL.Helkern a.k.a. SQL.Slammer a.k.a. Sapphire) during last weekend. Signature of corresponding exploit is known to SDS since May 2002, which is 9 months ago.

Worm.SQL.Helkern spreads via buffer overflow breach, known as "Unauthenticated Remote Compromise in MS SQL Server 2000". More on this is available at the following vendor site:
http://www.microsoft.com/technet/treeview/default.asp?url=/technet/security/bulletin/MS02-039.asp

Shadow Database Scanner (SDS) is able to recognize over one hundred fifty vulnerabilities and potential exploits of Domino, IBM DB2, MySQL, MS SQL, Postgres SQL, MiniSQL, and Oracle database servers, thus giving you complete and up-to date vision of your crucial data resources.
